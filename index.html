Mahatma Gandhi

The man who fears no truth has nothing to fear from lies.

Here's a time line of Mahatma Gandhi's life:

  • 1869 - Born on October 2 in Porbandar, India.
  • 1888 - Travels to London to study law.
  • 1893 - Moves to South Africa to work as a lawyer; begins his activism against racial discrimination.
  • 1906 - Initiates the first Satyagraha (non-violent protest) in South Africa against anti-Indian legislation.
  • 1915 - Returns to India and joins the Indian National Congress; begins to lead the struggle for India's independence.
  • 1919 - Leads a nationwide protest against the Rowlatt Act; the Jallianwala Bagh massacre occurs.
  • 1920-1922 - Leads the Non-Cooperation Movement, urging Indians to boycott British goods and institutions.
  • 1930 - Leads the Salt March, a 240-mile protest against the British salt tax.
  • 1942 - Launches the Quit India Movement, demanding an end to British rule.
  • 1947 - India gains independence from British rule on August 15; partition leads to the creation of India and Pakistan.
  • 1948 - Assassinated on January 30 by Nathuram Godse in New Delhi.
